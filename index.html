Introduction

Word-initial engma deletion is increasingly observed in younger Kunwinjku speakers, challenging traditional phonotactic constraints and expanding into previously restricted domains.

Method

Multi-generational elicitation sessions were conducted with:

  • 2 elder speakers
  • 2 younger speakers
  • Visual stimulus kit
  • Natural conversation recording

Previous Research

Marley (2022) hypothesized that engma deletion was restricted to closed-class morphemes. Recent observations suggest this constraint is weakening among younger speakers.

Results

1. Consistent engma deletion in younger speakers

2. Preservation in elder speech

3. Dialect awareness among speakers

4. Extension to sacred terms

Discussion

The study reveals:

  • Systematic generational variation
  • Changing phonotactic constraints
  • Complex interplay between age and dialect

Future Research
